LNP OS for Apple Silicon

To begin the installation process, download the ARM ISO file from our official website. Once downloaded, use Balena Etcher to flash the ISO onto a USB drive or other bootable media. Balena Etcher is easy to use and available for all major platforms. You can access it through the link below:

Balena Etcher - Official Website

After creating your bootable USB, plug it into your Apple Silicon device. Then, press and hold the power button until the boot options appear. From there, select "LNP OS Installer" to launch the installation environment.

LNP OS is based on a robust Linux foundation and is optimized for Apple Silicon hardware. There's no need to install additional drivers — the system will automatically detect and configure all compatible hardware components for you.

We recommend having at least 15–20GB of free storage and around 8GB of swap memory to ensure smooth performance, especially when running multiple applications or working with resource-intensive tasks.

Want to keep macOS? No problem. During the installation process, simply click the button labeled "Install LNP OS Alongside macOS". The installer will safely partition your drive, allowing both operating systems to coexist without any data loss.

Once installation is complete, remove the USB or installation media and reboot your device.

If you are using Dualboot, hold the power button to see Startup Option, and click on LNP OS.

Once you start you need to prepare your OS. Don't have any driver? Just open Device Manager, and them will search automatically.

Start your journey with LNP OS today and experience the freedom and flexibility of Linux on Apple hardware.
